    Thanks Elizabeth for clarifying. It's ok. It makes sense as different sites have different manpower and priorities.

So coming back to the user's request. For general understanding of the site's policy : we try to keep the OS as simple as possible, because CMS has a meta-package called HEP_OSLibs, that is thoroughly tested with CMS's bootstrap environment. It might sound paranoid but sometimes having different versions of different libraries (openSSL specially), different python, perl interpreters, etc versions will result in software crashing and people taking long before finding the culprit. So our WNs are SL6-base + HEP_OSLibs + Admin needs

The ideal is to tell users that if they want anything else, they should send in their job's sandbox, in a way that your job can use the extras from its own bootstrap. However we can make exceptions for too simple packages. I recognize that lately CentOS and other distributions are making an effort to become an skeleton of what is needed or less in the default installation. Probably the reason that your jobs worked under CentOS5 and now under CentOS6 are crashing for missing these basic things.

There's also the risk of your requested software to require dependencies that can cause problems in the wn-client bundle for example. So what I will do is to check dependencies for your list, which looks ok, and install the trivial packages.

I don't like the idea of 7z though, AFAIK is not really a Linux-standard tool and it deviates a bit of our definition of what's ok to install. I would prefer that it's not used at all, in our site, or sent in your job's bootstrap and used from there.

Thanks,
Samir
