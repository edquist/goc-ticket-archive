    Hey Xin,

Sorry, I was at Condor Week and wasn't able to look at this ticket.

Luckily, if we fix this, we would reduce the amount of BDII data for BNL by 3-4x, which might have avoided the issue this afternoon.

1) You want to add, in the [GIP] section,
cluster_name=BNL-ATLAS
Do this for every cluster.  The actual name doesn't matter - but what matters is having a cluster_name common to all the CEs.
2) Yes, you'll add a new other_ces variable to the [GIP] section also.
3) Yes, the document needs to be updated to say resource_name and resource_group_name instead of site_name.  I will do this in the document.

At Nebraska, we have 2 CEs on one cluster, red.unl.edu and gpn-husker.unl.edu.  Here's what our red.unl.edu [GIP] section looks like:

[GIP]
cluster_name = red.unl.edu
other_ces = gpn-husker.unl.edu

Here's what our gpn-husker.unl.edu [GIP] section looks like:

[GIP]
cluster_name = red.unl.edu
other_ces = red.unl.edu

Does this help?  If you do this for all the BNL clusters, all the ATLAS release and subcluster information will be the same, *greatly* reducing the volume of data.
