    [by Scott Teige]

The GOC has received several critical level alarms indicating degraded performance of MyOSG. This behavior is caused by a large number of sites querying MyOSG simultaneously near the beginning of the hour, in particular in2p3 and cern have an unusually large number of machines hitting MyOSG at the top of the hour. Cern is aware of the machines in question, the in2p3 machines are listed below. This problem is easily solved by changing the schedule of the cron jobs querying MyOSG to have a random offset while maintaining the current frequency.
